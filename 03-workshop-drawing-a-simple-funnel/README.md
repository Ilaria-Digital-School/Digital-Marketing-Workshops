# Workshop: drawing a simple funnel

THEORETICAL RECAP The conversion funnel (also called the marketing funnel) is a model that represents the journey a prospect takes from their very first contact with a brand all the way to becoming a customer — and potentially a loyal advocate. The classic simplified version breaks this journey into four stages: Awareness (the prospect discovers the brand or product), Consideration (they evaluate options and show interest), Conversion (they take the desired action, typically a purchase), and Retention/Loyalty (they return and recommend). At each stage, the audience naturally shrinks, which is why the shape is a funnel: many people enter at the top, fewer reach the bottom. A common mistake beginners make is treating the funnel as a strict linear process. In reality, prospects can jump between stages, exit and re-enter, or skip steps entirely. Another frequent error is confusing channels (where you communicate) with funnel stages (where the customer is mentally). For example, social media can be used at the Awareness stage AND at the Consideration stage depending on the content. A quick mini example: A user sees a sponsored Instagram post about a running shoe brand (Awareness). They click, browse the website and read reviews (Consideration). They add a pair to their cart and complete the purchase (Conversion). A week later, they receive a loyalty email with a discount for their next order (Retention). This simple sequence is the skeleton of virtually every digital marketing strategy you will build. --- WORKSHOP BRIEF Now that you have a solid understanding...
