# Workshop: combining marketing goals and channels

THEORETICAL RECAP Before diving into the workshop, let's consolidate the core concepts you've encountered so far. A digital marketing channel is a medium or platform through which a brand communicates with its audience — examples include social media, email, SEO, paid search (SEA), display advertising, and content marketing. A marketing goal, on the other hand, defines what you want to achieve: generating awareness, driving traffic, capturing leads, converting prospects into customers, or retaining existing clients. The classic framework used to map goals is the AIDA model (Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action), often extended with a Loyalty stage. The critical mental model to retain is this: not all channels serve all goals equally well. Paid social ads are powerful for awareness and retargeting, but poor for capturing high-intent buyers. SEO drives organic traffic over time but is slow for immediate conversions. Email marketing excels at nurturing and retention. A common beginner mistake is to pick channels based on personal familiarity rather than strategic fit — for example, investing heavily in Instagram when the actual goal is B2B lead generation, where LinkedIn would perform far better. Mini example: A startup launching a new SaaS tool wants to build brand awareness first, then convert trial users. A smart combination would be: YouTube pre-roll ads and LinkedIn sponsored content for awareness, a dedicated landing page optimized for SEO for traffic, and an automated email sequence for conversion and onboarding. Each channel is chosen because it serves a specific stage of the funnel. --- WORKSHOP BRIEF...
