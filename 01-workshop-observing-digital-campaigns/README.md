# Workshop: observing digital campaigns

THEORETICAL RECAP Digital marketing encompasses all marketing actions carried out through digital channels — search engines, social media, email, display advertising, and more. A digital campaign is a coordinated set of messages and actions deployed across one or more of these channels to achieve a specific goal: brand awareness, lead generation, sales conversion, or customer retention. Every campaign rests on four pillars: the target audience (who you are speaking to), the message (what you are communicating), the channel (where you are communicating), and the KPIs — Key Performance Indicators — (how you measure success). A common beginner mistake is to confuse the channel with the campaign itself. The channel (e.g., Instagram) is simply the medium; the campaign is the entire strategic construction around it. Another frequent error is ignoring the customer journey: a campaign rarely converts at first contact. Users typically move through awareness, consideration, and decision stages before acting. Mini example: A sports brand launches a new running shoe. On YouTube, they run a 30-second awareness video. On Google Search, they bid on keywords like 'best running shoes 2024.' On Instagram, they retarget users who visited their product page with a discount offer. Three channels, one campaign, one coherent objective. Keep this mental model in mind as you begin observing real campaigns in the wild. --- WORKSHOP BRIEF The goal of this workshop is to train your eye as a digital marketing analyst.
