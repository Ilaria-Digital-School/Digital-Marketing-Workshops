# Workshop: creating a dedicated Gmail address

THEORETICAL RECAP Before diving into the practical work, let's establish a clear mental model. In digital marketing, your work environment is your foundation. Just as a craftsman keeps their tools organized and separate from personal belongings, a digital marketer must maintain a clear distinction between personal and professional digital assets. A dedicated Gmail address is not just an email inbox — it is the central hub from which you will access Google's entire ecosystem: Google Analytics, Google Ads, Google Search Console, Google Tag Manager, Google My Business, and more. All of these tools are interconnected through a single Google account, which is why the account you create today must be purpose-built for your marketing practice. A common mistake among beginners is using a personal Gmail account for professional tools. This creates confusion between personal and professional data, complicates collaboration, and can lead to access issues when switching devices or sharing credentials with a team. Another frequent error is choosing a vague or unprofessional username that is difficult to recognize or remember. Best practice is to choose a clear, professional naming convention such as firstname.lastname.marketing@gmail.com or a variation that reflects your professional identity. Mini example: a learner named Sophie Martin creates sophie.martin.digitalmarketing@gmail.com. She uses this account exclusively to register for all marketing tools throughout the course. Every workshop deliverable, every tool access, every report — all tied to this single, clean professional identity.
